Benvenuto nella seconda parte della nostra guida ai verbi essenziali! Se nella [prima parte]({{ site.baseurl }}/inglese/da-zero/25-verbi-essenziali-parte-1/) abbiamo visto la routine e il movimento, qui entriamo nel vivo della **mente**, della **socialità avanzata** e della **creazione**.

Padroneggiare questi verbi ti permetterà di esprimere opinioni, studiare e costruire progetti. Ricorda di applicare i nostri [trucchi per ricordare le parole]({{ site.baseurl }}/inglese/da-zero/come-ricordare-parole-studiate/) per non dimenticare quello che impari oggi!

## 🗣️ Comunicazione & Socialità (Parte 2: 26-30)

### 26. Listen - Ascoltare
* *You never listen to me!*
* *I like to listen to music while working.*
* *Please, listen carefully.*
* *Are you listening to what I'm saying?* (Stai ascoltando quello che dico?)
* *Listen to your heart.* (Ascolta il tuo cuore.)

### 27. Hear - Udire / Sentire
* *I can't hear you, the music is too loud.*
* *Did you hear the news?*
* *I heard a strange noise.*
* *I'm sorry to hear that.* (Mi dispiace sapere questo.)
* *Can you hear me now?* (Mi senti ora?)

### 28. Meet - Incontrare / Conoscere
* *Nice to meet you.*
* *Let's meet at the bar at 8 p.m.*
* *I met my best friend at school.*
* *We are meeting for lunch.* (Ci incontriamo per pranzo.)
* *I'd like you to meet my brother.* (Vorrei presentarti mio fratello.)

### 29. Help - Aiutare
* *Can you help me with this bag?*
* *I am happy to help.*
* *She helps her mother every day.*
* *I can't help it.* (Non posso farci niente.)
* *Thank you for your help.* (Grazie per il tuo aiuto.)

### 30. Share - Condividere
* *I want to share this pizza with you.*
* *Please share this post with your friends.*
* *We share the same office.*
* *Share your ideas with the group.* (Condividi le tue idee col gruppo.)
* *He doesn't like sharing his toys.* (Non gli piace condividere i suoi giocattoli.)

> **🗣️ Dialogo: "The New Colleague"**
> **Tom:** Did you **hear** the news? We have a new colleague!
> **Sara:** Yes! I **met** him yesterday. He seems nice, but he doesn't speak Italian very well.
> **Tom:** Oh, I see. I will **talk** to him later and **ask** if he needs qualcosa.
> **Sara:** Good idea. I can **help** him **share** his documents on the computer. I will **answer** all his questions!
> **Tom:** Perfect, I think he will be happy to **listen** to your advice.

## 🧠 Mente & Apprendimento (31-40)

### 31. Read - Leggere
* *I read a book before going to bed.*
* *Have you read this article?*
* *She can read very fast.*
* *I can't read your handwriting.* (Non riesco a leggere la tua calligrafia.)
* *Read the instructions carefully.* (Leggi le istruzioni con attenzione.)

### 32. Write - Scrivere
* *Please write your name here.*
* *I am writing an email to my boss.*
* *He wrote a beautiful song.*
* *Write it down before you forget.* (Scrivilo prima di dimenticare.)
* *I haven't written to her for ages.* (Non le scrivo da una vita.)

### 33. Learn - Imparare
* *I want to learn English this year.*
* *Children learn very fast.*
* *I learned a lot from my mistakes.*
* *You're never too old to learn.* (Non si è mai troppo vecchi per imparare.)
* *I'm learning how to cook.* (Sto imparando a cucinare.)

### 34. Study - Studiare
* *She studies Medicine at the university.*
* *You need to study for the exam.*
* *I studied all night.*
* *Where did you study?* (Dove hai studiato?)
* *I need a quiet place to study.* (Ho bisogno di un posto calmo.)

### 35. Understand - Capire
* *Do you understand the grammar rule?*
* *I'm sorry, I don't understand.*
* *Now I understand why you are angry.*
* *Do you understand what I'm saying?* (Capisci cosa sto dicendo?)
* *It's difficult to understand him.* (È difficile capirlo.)

### 36. Remember - Ricordare
* *I will always remember this day.*
* *Do you remember his name?*
* *Please remember to lock the door.*
* *I can't remember where I put my keys.* (Non ricordo dove ho messo le chiavi.)
* *As far as I remember...* (Per quanto io ricordi...)

### 37. Forget - Dimenticare
* *Don't forget your keys!*
* *I completely forgot about the meeting.*
* *I will never forget you.*
* *I forgot what I was going to say.* (Ho dimenticato cosa stavo per dire.)
* *Forget about it.* (Dimenticatene/Non farci caso.)

### 38. Believe - Credere
* *I believe in you, you can do it!*
* *Do you believe in ghosts?*
* *I can't believe my eyes!*
* *Believe me, it's true.* (Credimi, è vero.)
* *I believe she is right.* (Credo che lei abbia ragione.)

### 39. Choose - Scegliere
* *You have to choose one color.*
* *It's hard to choose the best option.*
* *I chose to stay at home.*
* *There's so much to choose from.* (C'è così tanto tra cui scegliere.)
* *Choose wisely.* (Scegli saggiamente.)

### 40. Decide - Decidere
* *We decided to go to the beach.*
* *I can't decide what to order.*
* *She decided to change her job.*
* *I've decided to quit smoking.* (Ho deciso di smettere di fumare.)
* *It's up to you to decide.* (Sta a te decidere.)

## 🛠️ Creazione & Cambiamento (41-50)

### 41. Make - Fare / Creare (fisico)
* *She makes beautiful clothes.*
* *Can you make a cake for my birthday?*
* *Don't make a noise!*
* *You make me happy.* (Mi rendi felice.)
* *I need to make a phone call.* (Devo fare una telefonata.)

### 42. Do - Fare (azione/dovere)
* *You must do your homework.*
* *I will do the dishes tonight.*
* *What are you doing?*
* *I'll do my best.* (Farò del mio meglio.)
* *Do me a favor.* (Fammi un favore.)

### 43. Build - Costruire
* *They will build a new hospital here.*
* *Rome wasn't built in a day.*
* *He wants to build a successful business.*
* *Birds build nests in spring.* (Gli uccelli costruiscono nidi in primavera.)
* *We need to build a better future.* (Dobbiamo costruire un futuro migliore.)

### 44. Open - Aprire
* *Please open the window, it's hot.*
* *What time does the shop open?*
* *He opened the box slowly.*
* *Open your eyes.* (Apri gli occhi.)
* *She opened a new restaurant.* (Ha aperto un nuovo ristorante.)

### 45. Close - Chiudere
* *Don't forget to close the door.*
* *The supermarket closes at 9 p.m.*
* *Please, close your eyes.*
* *The library is closed on Sundays.* (La biblioteca è chiusa la domenica.)
* *Close the window, it's cold.* (Chiudi la finestra, fa freddo.)

### 46. Start - Iniziare
* *The movie starts in ten minutes.*
* *I will start a new diet on Monday.*
* *It started to rain.*
* *Let's start from the beginning.* (Iniziamo dall'inizio.)
* *I can't start the engine.* (Non riesco ad avviare il motore.)

### 47. Stop - Fermare / Smettere
* *Please, stop talking!*
* *The bus stopped suddenly.*
* *I want to stop smoking.*
* *Stop it! You're hurting me.* (Smettila! Mi stai facendo male.)
* *Nothing can stop us now.* (Niente può fermarci ora.)

### 48. Change - Cambiare
* *I need to change my clothes.*
* *People can change.*
* *Can you change a 50 euro note?*
* *Things have changed a lot.* (Le cose sono cambiate molto.)
* *The weather is changing.* (Il tempo sta cambiando.)

### 49. Grow - Crescere
* *Children grow up very fast.*
* *My grandfather grows tomatoes in his garden.*
* *The company is growing quickly.*
* *I want to grow my hair long.* (Voglio far crescere i capelli lunghi.)
* *The problem is growing.* (Il problema sta crescendo.)

### 50. Try - Provare / Tentare
* *You should try this cake, it's delicious!*
* *I will try to fix the computer.*
* *Keep trying, don't give up!*
* *I've never tried sushi.* (Non ho mai provato il sushi.)
* *At least I tried.* (Almeno ci ho provato.)
