## 🗣️ Comunicazione & Socialità (26-30)

7. **Listen / Hear:** Ascoltare (attenzione) vs Udire (percezione).
   * *I like to listen to music while working.*
   * *I can't hear you, the music is too loud.*
   * *Listen to your heart.* (Ascolta il tuo cuore.)
   * *I'm sorry to hear that.* (Mi dispiace sapere questo.)

8. **Meet / Help / Share:**
   * *Nice to meet you.*
   * *Can you help me with this bag?*
   * *I want to share this pizza with you.*
   * *I can't help it.* (Non posso farci niente.)

> **🗣️ Dialogo: "The New Colleague"**
> **Tom:** Did you **hear** the news? We have a new colleague!
> **Sara:** Yes! I **met** him yesterday. He seems nice. I will **talk** to him later and **ask** if he needs something.
> **Tom:** Perfect, I think he will be happy to **listen** to your advice.

## 🧠 Mente & Apprendimento (31-40)

Questi sono i verbi che usi per navigare nel mondo delle idee e dello studio.



* **Read / Write:** *I read a book before bed. Please write your name here.*
* **Learn / Study:** *I want to learn English this year. You need to study for the exam.*
* **Understand:** *Do you understand the grammar rule? Now I understand why you are angry.*
* **Remember / Forget:** *I will always remember this day. Don't forget your keys!*
* **Believe / Choose / Decide:** *I believe in you! It's hard to choose the best option. We decided to go to the beach.*

## 🛠️ Creazione & Cambiamento (41-50)

Qui arriviamo ai verbi "forti". Attenzione alla differenza tra *Make* e *Do*, una delle sfide più comuni per noi italiani!



* **Make / Do:** *She makes beautiful clothes. You must do your homework. You make me happy.*
* **Build / Open / Close:** *Rome wasn't built in a day. What time does the shop open? Close the door, please.*
* **Start / Stop:** *The movie starts in ten minutes. Please, stop talking!*
* **Change / Grow / Try:** *People can change. Children grow up very fast. You should try this cake!*

> **📖 Mini-Storia: "The Important Exam"**
> Leo wants to **learn** German. Every afternoon he **studies** grammar and **writes** vocabulary. Yesterday, he had a test. He was afraid to **forget** the words, but he **remembered** everything. When the teacher gave him the result, he couldn't **believe** it: he passed! He **decided** to celebrate with pizza!
