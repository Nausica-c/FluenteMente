Se le parole sono i mattoni della lingua, i **verbi** sono il cemento e il motore. Senza di loro, le tue frasi resterebbero immobili. Per un italiano che inizia da zero, imparare i verbi giusti significa passare dal "sopravvivere" al comunicare davvero.

In questo primo approfondimento analizziamo i verbi di **Routine (1-10)**, **Movimento (11-20)** e l'inizio della **Comunicazione (21-25)**. Se vuoi un ripasso generale prima di iniziare, dai un'occhiata alle nostre [100 parole inglesi più usate]({{ site.baseurl }}/inglese/da-zero/100-parole-inglesi-piu-usate/).

## ☕ Sopravvivenza & Routine (1-10)

Questi sono i verbi che descrivono ciò che fai dal momento in cui apri gli occhi.

### 1. Wake (up) - Svegliarsi
* *I usually wake up at 7 a.m.*
* *She woke up late today.*
* *What time do you wake up on Sundays?*
* *Don't wake the baby up!* (Non svegliare il bambino!)
* *I woke up with a headache.* (Mi sono svegliato con il mal di testa.)

### 2. Wash - Lavare / Lavarsi
* *Please wash your hands before dinner.*
* *I wash my car every weekend.*
* *Did you wash the dishes?*
* *You should wash that cut.* (Dovresti lavare quel taglio.)
* *I need to wash my hair.* (Devo lavarmi i capelli.)

### 3. Dress - Vestire / Vestirsi
* *He dresses very elegantly for work.*
* *I need five minutes to dress.*
* *She dressed the baby warmly.*
* *Hurry up and get dressed!* (Sbrigati e vestiti!)
* *She always dresses in blue.* (Si veste sempre di blu.)

### 4. Eat - Mangiare
* *We eat pizza on Friday nights.*
* *I don't eat meat, I am vegetarian.*
* *What did you eat for lunch?*
* *Do you want something to eat?* (Vuoi qualcosa da mangiare?)
* *I ate too much at the party.* (Ho mangiato troppo alla festa.)

### 5. Drink - Bere
* *You must drink a lot of water.*
* *I like to drink tea in the afternoon.*
* *He doesn't drink alcohol.*
* *What would you like to drink?* (Cosa vorresti bere?)
* *She drank the juice quickly.* (Ha bevuto il succo velocemente.)

### 6. Work - Lavorare / Funzionare
* *I work in a big office.*
* *She works hard every day.*
* *Where do you work?*
* *This remote doesn't work.* (Questo telecomando non funziona.)
* *I'm working on a new project.* (Sto lavorando a un nuovo progetto.)

### 7. Play - Giocare / Suonare
* *The children play in the garden.*
* *Can you play the guitar?*
* *We play football on Saturdays.*
* *Stop playing with your phone!* (Smettila di giocare col telefono!)
* *She plays the violin in an orchestra.* (Suona il violino in un'orchestra.)

### 8. Buy - Comprare
* *I need to buy some milk.*
* *Where did you buy those shoes?*
* *She buys a lot of books.*
* *Money can't buy happiness.* (I soldi non comprano la felicità.)
* *I'm going to buy a new car.* (Comprerò una macchina nuova.)

### 9. Pay - Pagare
* *Can I pay by credit card?*
* *I will pay for the dinner.*
* *How much did you pay for this?*
* *I have to pay the rent today.* (Devo pagare l'affitto oggi.)
* *Did you pay the electricity bill?* (Hai pagato la bolletta della luce?)

### 10. Sleep - Dormire
* *I usually sleep for eight hours.*
* *The baby is sleeping, be quiet.*
* *I didn't sleep well last night.*
* *Go to sleep, it's late.* (Vai a dormire, è tardi.)
* *I slept until 10 a.m.* (Ho dormito fino alle 10.)

> **📖 Mini-Storia: "A Busy Morning"**
> Every day, Mark **wakes up** at 6:00 a.m. First, he **washes** his face and **dresses** quickly. He doesn't have much time to **eat** a big breakfast, so he only **drinks** a coffee. Then, he goes to **work**. After work, he goes to the supermarket to **buy** food and **pays** at the cashier. In the evening, he **plays** video games to relax, and finally, he goes to **sleep**.

## ✈️ Movimento & Viaggio (11-20)

### 11. Walk - Camminare
* *I walk to school every day.*
* *Let's walk in the park.*
* *She is walking the dog.*
* *I walked 10,000 steps today.* (Ho fatto 10.000 passi oggi.)
* *We can walk home from here.* (Possiamo camminare fino a casa da qui.)

### 12. Run - Correre
* *Don't run in the hospital!*
* *I run 5 kilometers every morning.*
* *He had to run to catch the bus.*
* *The water is still running.* (L'acqua sta ancora scorrendo.)
* *I'm running late!* (Sono in ritardo!)

### 13. Drive - Guidare
* *Can you drive a manual car?*
* *Please drive safely.*
* *I drove to Rome yesterday.*
* *My father taught me how to drive.* (Mio padre mi ha insegnato a guidare.)
* *It's a long drive to the mountains.* (È un lungo viaggio in auto.)

### 14. Fly - Volare / Viaggiare in aereo
* *We fly to London tomorrow.*
* *I am afraid to fly.*
* *The bird flew away.*
* *I prefer flying to driving.* (Preferisco volare che guidare.)
* *The pilot flies big planes.* (Il pilota guida grandi aerei.)

### 15. Travel - Viaggiare
* *They love to travel the world.*
* *I travel for work very often.*
* *Are you traveling alone?*
* *I want to travel across Asia.* (Voglio viaggiare attraverso l'Asia.)
* *Light travels faster than sound.* (La luce viaggia più veloce del suono.)

### 16. Arrive - Arrivare
* *The train arrives at 10:00 p.m.*
* *Call me when you arrive home.*
* *They arrived late to the party.*
* *What time do we arrive in Paris?* (A che ora arriviamo a Parigi?)
* *The guests have finally arrived.* (Gli ospiti sono finalmente arrivati.)

### 17. Leave - Partire / Lasciare
* *What time does the bus leave?*
* *Don't leave your bag here.*
* *I have to leave now.*
* *Leave me alone!* (Lasciami in pace!)
* *He left his umbrella on the train.* (Ha lasciato l'ombrello sul treno.)

### 18. Stay - Rimanere / Alloggiare
* *We will stay at a nice hotel.*
* *Please, stay with me.*
* *I decided to stay at home.*
* *How long are you going to stay?* (Quanto tempo rimarrai?)
* *Stay safe!* (Stai al sicuro!)

### 19. Bring - Portare (qui)
* *Don't forget to bring your umbrella!*
* *Can I bring a friend to the party?*
* *He brought me a beautiful gift.*
* *Could you bring me the bill?* (Potresti portarmi il conto?)
* *The news brought us great joy.* (La notizia ci ha portato gioia.)

### 20. Move - Muoversi / Traslocare
* *Please, move your chair a little.*
* *I will move to a new house next month.*
* *Don't move!*
* *The story moved me to tears.* (La storia mi ha commosso.)
* *Let's move to the next topic.* (Passiamo al prossimo argomento.)

## 🗣️ Comunicazione & Socialità (Parte 1: 21-25)

### 21. Speak - Parlare (lingue)
* *I speak Italian and English.*
* *Could you speak more slowly, please?*
* *He is speaking on the phone.*
* *Actions speak louder than words.* (Le azioni contano più delle parole.)
* *I spoke to her yesterday.* (Le ho parlato ieri.)

### 22. Talk - Conversare
* *Let's talk about your future.*
* *She talks too much!*
* *I need to talk to the manager.*
* *We need to talk.* (Dobbiamo parlare.)
* *Stop talking and work.* (Smetti di parlare e lavora.)

### 23. Ask - Chiedere
* *Can I ask you a question?*
* *If you don't know, just ask.*
* *He asked me for money.*
* *Don't be afraid to ask for help.* (Non aver paura di chiedere aiuto.)
* *I asked her to go out with me.* (Le ho chiesto di uscire con me.)

### 24. Answer - Rispondere
* *Please answer the phone.*
* *I can't answer this question.*
* *She didn't answer my email.*
* *Wait for my answer.* (Aspetta la mia risposta.)
* *He finally answered the door.* (Ha finalmente risposto alla porta.)

### 25. Call - Chiamare / Telefonare
* *I will call you later tonight.*
* *Please call a taxi for me.*
* *What do you call this in English?*
* *Someone is calling your name.* (Qualcuno sta chiamando il tuo nome.)
* *Call me if you need anything.* (Chiamami se hai bisogno di qualcosa.)
